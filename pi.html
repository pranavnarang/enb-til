GTM Pipeline Integrity

Turing (GTM Pipeline Integrity) is a project that allows Enbridge to continuously drive improvements to pipeline integrity and safety by bringing multiple field monitoring and assessment data in one place, and analyzing where engineers need to pay attention.

“ Encouraging innovation is a vital component of directing our own future. Innovation is about looking differently at what we need to achieve, and asking how else we can get there. ”

- Trevor Grams, Director - R&D

What is GTM Pipeline Integrity

Turing brings together geospatial data from multiple field monitoring and assessment activities to identify which sections of the pipeline are at higher failure risk and the causes behind the risk. Turing not only helps identify threat management actions needed to reduce rupture risk, but also data management actions needed to increase confidence in risk assessments, so Enbridge can focus its integrity effort where it is needed most. Turing applies diagnostic algorithms on data for every segment of the pipeline network to identify missing data, inaccurate data, or old data, helping the user focus on data that is in most need of cleanup.

Impact of the Project

Better Data Confidence

Common data pools to answer multiple question via “single source of truth” – Technology to identify and fix data gaps

Decision Effectiveness

Enable efficient management of field work and ensure risks are addressed properly.

Personnel Time

Reduce personnel time gathering and processing data; improving productivity and focus on strategic priorities.

Improved Analysis

Continuous risk assessment using machine learning and automated data workflows leads to better field productivity and aligns with existing field improvement efforts.
