Terminal Optimization

Terminal Optimization (TO) is one of several ways that Enbridge is optimizing the world’s longest and most complex crude oil and liquids network—supporting the energy sector by getting Canadian crude oil to market, and helping Canadians reap the benefits of a natural resources economy.

“ There are literally thousands of flow paths through a terminal. And every terminal is designed and operated differently. Our terminals are our areas of opportunity. ”

-Tony Khoo, Lab Head - Calgary

What is Terminal Optimization

Terminal Optimization provides real-time information about asset utilization, commodity movement and other salient features to the terminal operators themselves and to the Infrastructure Planning and Capacity Management teams who work to resolve bottlenecks, maximize throughput and ensure system-wide efficiency.

Impact of the Project

Improved Throughput

Increased throughput of commodity by recommending the best flow path; Increased asset utilization for capacity constrained lines.

Increased Productivity

Reduced disruption of planned activities due to ad-hoc data collection requests and analysis on historical rate loss; Increased productivity of operators by predicting flow paths.

Reduced Maintenance Costs

Optimized maintenance expenditure by identifying critical maintenance of assets; Reduce emergency maintenance costs if commodity is flowing through faulty pump.

Regulatory Compliance

Ensuring that commodity flow sequence is compliant; Avoiding flow to older assets; Lower incidents of deactivation and re-activation.
